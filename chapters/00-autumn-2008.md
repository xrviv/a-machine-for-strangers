> **Fiction notice:** This chapter uses historical fiction. Nakamoto’s imagined thoughts are not presented as fact. The dated events and linked sources are evidence.

Autumn 2008 was a period of financial collapse and emergency intervention. Lehman
Brothers filed for bankruptcy on 15 September 2008; the following day, the Federal
Reserve supported AIG as financial stress intensified. By year’s end, the Federal
Reserve’s target interest-rate range had fallen to 0–0.25 percent.

- [Federal Reserve History: The Great Recession and Its Aftermath](https://www.federalreservehistory.org/essays/great-recession-and-its-aftermath)
- [FDIC: Crisis and Response, 2008–2013](https://www.fdic.gov/sites/default/files/2024-03/crisis-response.pdf)

On 31 October 2008, an author using the name Satoshi Nakamoto published _Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System_.

This project does not claim to know why Nakamoto wrote Bitcoin or what Nakamoto believed. It asks a narrower question:

> What problems does the original software suggest its author was trying to solve?

The answer begins with code—not a price chart, legend, or identity claim.
